### Common model conventions

- Revenue is built from operating units (accounts and blended annual contract value), not a top-down market-growth percentage. Forecasts represent an HCP base case and are not management guidance.
- Entry ownership equals check divided by post-money valuation. Exit ownership equals entry ownership multiplied by one minus cumulative dilution. Seed-stage companies (Thena, Dock, Kula) use a 40% dilution assumption to reflect additional financing rounds expected before exit; Series A and Series B companies use 35%, matching the reference file's convention.
- Exit enterprise value equals 2032 revenue multiplied by the selected EV/LTM revenue multiple. HCP proceeds equal exit enterprise value multiplied by exit ownership. MOIC equals proceeds divided by check. The model excludes preference effects, taxes, fees, secondary sales, and follow-on reserves.
- Public-comps multiples are snapshots as of July 18, 2026 from the linked StockAnalysis pages, whose financial statistics cite S&P Global Market Intelligence. Multiples reused directly from the reference file (`HCP_10_Investment_Memos_2026-07-18.md`) for Oracle, SAP, Workday, Manhattan Associates, and Shopify are noted where used; all other tickers were fetched fresh for this batch.
- Downside uses 60% of base exit revenue and one turn less on the multiple, with a 1.0x floor. Upside uses 150% of base exit revenue and one turn more. Dilution is held constant.
- Competitive tables use **X** only where the capability is verified, **Partial** where the product covers only part of the workflow, **No evidence** where public material does not support the claim, and **Undisclosed** for unavailable commercial or capital data.

### Product description

Tofu is an agentic demand-generation platform for B2B marketing teams. It generates and launches hyper-personalized campaigns across email, landing pages, and paid ads from a shared brand and product knowledge base, and positions itself as consolidating point tools (personalization, landing-page builders, ad-copy generators, and content localization) into one workflow. The company's own marketing states campaigns can ship "8x faster." Its wedge is not content generation alone — many tools generate marketing copy — but agent orchestration across the full campaign lifecycle: brief, generate, localize, launch, and iterate from performance data.

### Thesis: why invest

Tofu raised a $12m Series A in February 2025, led by SignalFire with HubSpot Ventures, Tau Ventures, Correlation Ventures, and existing investors Index Ventures, Stage 2 Capital, and Liquid 2 Ventures participating; this followed a $5m seed led by Index Ventures. The company-reported growth is strong for this stage: a 12x increase in platform revenue and a 36x increase in content generated over the prior year, plus named enterprise customers RingCentral, Check Point, and Bluecore. These are company-disclosed figures from the financing announcement and have not been independently audited by HCP.

The category is crowded — HubSpot, 6sense, Mutiny, and a wave of AI content startups all compete for GTM budget — but Tofu's control point is the campaign-orchestration layer that sits above individual channel tools and pulls from a persistent brand/product knowledge base. If that knowledge base becomes the system of record for how a company talks about itself across channels, switching costs rise with every campaign shipped.

**What must be true:** the 12x revenue growth must continue from a larger base rather than decelerate immediately post-Series A; enterprise customers must expand seat and channel usage rather than churn after initial campaigns; and Tofu must maintain differentiation as HubSpot and other incumbents add native AI campaign generation.

**Next-round milestones:** $15m ARR, 50+ customers above $50k ACV, net revenue retention above 120%, and at least three enterprise logos referenceable for a Series B raise.

### Founder bet

The bet is a GTM-experienced founding team (EJ Cho as CEO, two additional named co-founders) that identified martech tool sprawl as a wedge and built an agent layer rather than a single-purpose generator. HubSpot Ventures' participation as a strategic investor is a signal worth testing directly — it could indicate a future acquisition or channel partnership, or simply financial return-seeking. Diligence should confirm the founding team's prior operating experience (undisclosed in public sources) and probe whether HubSpot's involvement constrains Tofu's own platform ambitions.

### Product description

Default is an AI infrastructure platform for revenue teams that unifies the inbound go-to-market stack: a real-time GTM data layer connecting CRM and marketing-automation systems, "Dot," an AI revenue agent that plans and executes multi-step GTM work, and a set of orchestration tools covering forms, meeting scheduling, lead routing and assignment, and waterfall enrichment. The wedge is consolidation: rather than stitching together a scheduling tool, a routing tool, an enrichment vendor, and a forms product, Default bundles the data layer and the workflow logic that connects them, with an agent able to act across that stack.

### Thesis: why invest

Default disclosed $6.6m in seed funding in a November 2023 announcement, then closed a Series A led by 8VC with participation from Craft Ventures and angel investor Jack Altman, bringing cumulative disclosed funding to $20m. The exact Series A tranche size and closing date were not separately broken out in the company's own announcement (the post frames the raise alongside a product launch rather than as a standalone financing release); HCP treats the $20m cumulative figure as the reliable disclosed data point. Named customers include Perplexity, Owner, and Cortex, and the company states it has served "hundreds of the fastest-growing companies" over 2.5 years of operation — a company claim, not independently verified.

The category (inbound routing, scheduling, enrichment) has several point-solution incumbents (Chili Piper for routing/scheduling, Clay for enrichment), but Default's bet is that a unified data layer plus an agent that can execute across all of them is more valuable than any single best-of-breed tool, particularly as AI agents increasingly need write access to a coherent GTM data model rather than a patchwork of point APIs.

**What must be true:** customers must consolidate multiple point tools into Default rather than layering it on top of existing tools; the Dot agent must demonstrably reduce manual RevOps work rather than add another dashboard to monitor; and Default must defend against HubSpot, Salesforce, and Clay each extending into adjacent workflow territory.

**Next-round milestones:** $15m ARR, 100+ customers with three or more modules adopted (routing, scheduling, enrichment, or agent workflows), net revenue retention above 120%, and at least two enterprise logos above $150k ACV.

### Founder bet

The bet is a single publicly named technical/product founder (Nico Ferreyra) who has iterated the product from a narrower "AI-powered inbound sales funnel" (per the 2023 seed announcement) into a broader GTM orchestration platform by 2025 — evidence of willingness to reposition based on market feedback. Diligence should establish the full founding team (public sources name only Ferreyra), prior operating experience, and technical leadership depth given the platform's ambition to run agentic workflows against production revenue data.

### Product description

Thena is a customer support platform purpose-built for B2B teams that live in Slack, Microsoft Teams, in-app chat, and email rather than a standalone helpdesk. It brings enterprise support workflows — SLA policies, escalations, ownership rules, AI-based routing, and an AI web-chat agent — directly into the channels customers and support teams already use, positioning against dedicated ticketing tools that require customers to leave their existing workflow. Published pricing (as of this research) lists Starter and Standard tiers at $79 per user per month (up to 10 seats, 1,000 tickets/month, Slack and email) and an Enterprise tier from $119 per user per month with Microsoft Teams support, enterprise APIs, and additional security controls.

### Thesis: why invest

Thena has raised $7.8m in disclosed seed-stage funding across three rounds (most recently $600k in December 2023), including a $5m seed co-led by Lightspeed India and Sequoia and a $2.2m pre-seed co-led by Pear VC and Tenacity Venture Capital. The company's site names Clarify as a customer, and its solutions pages reference logos including Vercel, Amplitude, Etsy, and LaunchDarkly as company claims. A third-party data aggregator (Latka) reports an estimated $20m ARR and a $60m valuation as of 2024; HCP treats this figure as an unverified third-party estimate, not a company disclosure, and does not use it directly as the entry valuation in the return model below.

If the Latka-reported ARR is directionally accurate, Thena would be unusually capital-efficient relative to its $7.8m raised, and the absence of a fresh institutional round since December 2023 could reflect deliberate capital discipline rather than weak demand. The core bet is that Slack-native delivery is a durable wedge against Zendesk, Freshdesk, and Intercom for B2B companies whose support already happens inside collaboration tools, and that SLA/escalation depth keeps enterprise buyers from outgrowing the product.

**What must be true:** the reported ARR and customer growth must be verifiable in a data room; the company must show it can retain and expand logos like Vercel and LaunchDarkly as they scale support headcount; and Slack/Teams platform policy must remain favorable to third-party ticketing integrations.

**Next-round milestones:** verified $20m+ ARR, net revenue retention above 115%, 100+ paying enterprise accounts, and a priced institutional round that resolves the current funding-history gap.

### Founder bet

Four named co-founders with no further public operating background disclosed. The team has sustained the product through multiple seed tranches since 2022 and, if the third-party ARR estimate is even directionally correct, has built meaningful revenue on a small capital base — a potential signal of strong founder-market fit for a niche (Slack-native support) that larger platforms have not prioritized. Diligence should independently confirm founder backgrounds, current cap table, and the true revenue base before relying on any third-party estimate.

### Principal risks and why invest anyway

- **Aggressive TAM penetration:** the 20% base-case TAM penetration assumption is high and requires Gradient Labs to become the clear category leader in regulated-fintech AI customer operations rather than one of several credible vendors. Stress-test the model at half this penetration before finalizing terms.

Confirm U.S. regulatory-guardrail readiness (state-by-state lending and consumer-protection rules differ materially from UK/EU frameworks) before assuming the same growth rate translates.
- **Horizontal-vendor catch-up:** Decagon, Sierra, and Ada are well-capitalized and could add financial-services-specific compliance guardrails faster than Gradient Labs can out-execute on breadth. The defense is genuine regulatory depth, not first-mover advantage alone — require a technical review of the guardrail architecture, not just the marketing claim.
- **Why invest anyway:** this is the strongest fundamental combination in the batch — a founding team with direct, hard-to-replicate regulatory-operations experience, credible and fast-growing blue-chip fintech customers, a genuine compliance moat, and Series A (not yet Series B) pricing that leaves real ownership available for a $2.5m check.

## Investment committee conclusion

HCP should prioritize Gradient Labs and Tofu for direct diligence: both combine credible disclosed growth signals, real founder-market fit, and Series A pricing that leaves meaningful ownership available for a $1.0m-$2.5m check. Default, Thena, and Sequence merit standard diligence tracks — each has a real product wedge and a specific, resolvable information gap (founding-team disclosure, third-party ARR verification, or customer-cohort detail) that should be closed before a term sheet. Dock is a Watch: the underlying unit economics and customer roster are attractive, but a three-year fundraising silence is disqualifying until resolved. Kula is a Pass on current public evidence, primarily because of an even longer, four-year funding-disclosure gap, though it should be revisited promptly if the company surfaces a new financing event. Campfire, Metaview, and Doss are Price-sensitive: all three are legitimately strong businesses that have already re-priced into Series B territory (Campfire and Doss at disclosed valuations, Metaview at an assumed one), which structurally caps the base-case return achievable at this check size — HCP should pursue only with improved terms, not at the generic disclosed or assumed post-money.

No memo should advance on public evidence alone. The next step is a standardized data request covering cap table and terms, monthly revenue and gross margin, customer or user cohorts, acquisition channels, retention, security and compliance, pipeline, product telemetry, and reference calls. For Dock and Kula specifically, the first diligence step is simply confirming current operating status and any unannounced financing before any further underwriting work is justified.
